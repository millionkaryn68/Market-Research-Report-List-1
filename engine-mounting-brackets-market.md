<p><p>Engine mounting brackets secure engines to vehicle frames, reducing vibrations and ensuring stability. Inline engines use simpler brackets due to their straight cylinder alignment, common in compact cars. V-type engines, with cylinders in a V-shape, require more complex brackets for balance, often found in trucks and performance vehicles. W-type engines, with three or four cylinder banks, demand highly specialized brackets for their compact yet powerful design, used in luxury and high-performance cars. Flat engines, with horizontally opposed cylinders, use unique brackets for their low center of gravity, popular in sports cars and motorcycles.</p></p>
