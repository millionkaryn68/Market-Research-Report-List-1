<p><p>The seawater desalination reverse osmosis membrane market primarily includes two types: composite membranes and asymmetric membranes. Composite membranes consist of multiple layers, combining various materials to enhance salt rejection and permeability, leading to improved efficiency in desalination processes. In contrast, asymmetric membranes have a single-layer structure with varying porosity, offering a balance between mechanical strength and filtration performance. Both types are essential in addressing water scarcity by enabling the efficient conversion of seawater to potable water.</p></p>
